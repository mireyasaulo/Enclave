Enclave Wiki · Newcomer Tutorial

From sign-up and browsing to writing entries with AI assist, patrol review, and admin tooling — one page covers everything.

0Welcome

Enclave Wiki is a collaborative encyclopedia for AI character creation. Anyone can browse, signed-in users can create and edit character entries — and what you write isn't just descriptive text, it's a structured character recipe that an AI uses to roleplay that character.

Workflow-wise it works like Wikipedia:

  • All content is collaboratively editable, with full version history
  • Newcomers' edits enter a review queue first; patrollers approve them before they go public
  • You get diffs, reverts, page protection, and reporting — the standard collab toolkit

What makes it different from Wikipedia is that each entry is the AI's "soul". The fields you fill — name, bio, personality, expert domains, dialog scenes — get bundled into a structured recipe and fed to a large model. Entry quality directly determines AI behavior.

💡 Tip

Not sure where to start? Jump to the next section — "5-minute quickstart" — and walk through it once. All the concepts will fall into place.

15-minute quickstart User

1

Register

Click "Register" top-right, choose username (≥2 chars) and password (≥6 chars). New accounts start at the newcomer tier.

2

Browse a character

Back on the home page, click any card to open a character — see what a complete entry looks like.

3

Try search

Top search bar, type a keyword, press Enter. Search covers name, relationship, bio, and personality fields.

4

Make a private character

Sidebar → "My private characters" → "Create". Fill in a minimal version (name + bio + relationship), then click "AI generate" to fill the rest.

5

Submit a wiki edit

Find a public entry, click "Edit", change a small detail, write a ≥10-character edit summary, submit. Wait for patroller review.

💡 Tip

While you're a newcomer, every public-entry edit needs review. Once you've accumulated a track record and the system auto-promotes you to autoconfirmed, low-risk edits go live immediately.

2Sign up & sign in User

Register

Visit /register and provide:

  • Username: at least 2 characters, public, used to attribute your edits
  • Password: at least 6 characters, only visible to you
  • Confirm password: must match

After registering, your account starts at the newcomer tier (newcomer). You can edit immediately, but every public-entry edit enters the review queue.

Sign in

Visit /login. Two options:

  • Username + password: standard
  • Email code: a verification code is sent to your registered email; useful if you've forgotten your password

If you visit a page that requires sign-in, you'll be redirected to /login?redirect=... and bounced back automatically after signing in.

Account settings

Click your username top-right → "Account settings". You can:

  • Change password (requires email-code confirmation)
  • View / verify / change your email
  • See your profile: current role tier (newcomer/autoconfirmed/patroller/admin), total edits, approved-edit count, reverted count, and when you were auto-confirmed
⚠️ Note

Email is your only password-recovery channel. Bind it as soon as you register — without it, you can't recover a forgotten password without admin intervention.

3Browsing entries User

Character directory (home page)

The home page / lists all public characters, sorted by popularity / recent activity. Each card shows the avatar, name, relationship type, and the first lines of the bio.

Anatomy of a character page

Click into a character to see:

  • Header: avatar, name, relationship-type badge (friend / family / mentor / expert / custom), lifecycle badge (active / pending create / deleted / pending delete)
  • Body tabs:

    • "History": all revisions and diffs
    • "Talk": discussions related to the entry
  • Right-side actions: ⭐ Watch / unwatch, ✏️ Edit (sign-in required), 🗑️ Request delete (sign-in required), protection-status indicator

Stable vs current view

An entry can have two views:

  • Stable: what everyone sees by default — the most recent approved revision
  • Current: includes pending unreviewed edits — visible only to the editor and patrollers

So: right after you edit you'll see your changes (current view), but other visitors still see the old stable version until your edit is approved.

4Search User

The top search box on every page leads to /search?q=keyword.

What's indexed

  • Character name
  • Relationship description ("my best friend" etc.)
  • Bio
  • Personality

Result ranking

Results are sorted by relevance score, each with a 0–100 percentile shown. Click any result to open the character page.

💡 Tip

Search is open to unsigned-in visitors — perfect for "kick the tires" traffic.

5Creating & editing entries User

This is the heart of the wiki. All public characters are created and maintained through the same form.

5.1 Create a new entry

Sidebar → "Create character", or visit /create. The form has 6 sections:

SectionKey fieldsNotes
① BasicsName, avatar, bio, relationship type, relationship descriptionName and bio are mandatory; they're how others identify the character
② Dialog styleSpeaking style, catchphrases, emotional toneDetermines the AI's voice
③ Core logicValues, decision patterns, taboosThe character's "brain" — how they reason in edge cases
④ Trigger scenesTypical openers, role-play setupsWhen this character should be summoned
⑤ MemoryBackstory, key experiencesWhat the character "remembers"
⑥ Social paramsExpert domains, interaction preferencesAffects recommendation and search matching

5.2 AI one-click generation

Each section has an "AI generate" button at the top right that calls a large model to fill in empty fields.

💡 Recommended workflow

Fill name + bio + relationship manually first (your three anchor points), then click "Generate all" — the AI uses these anchors to fill every other empty field. By default it only fills empties, never overwrites what you've written.

Key parameters:

  • Rate limit: 15 generations per user per hour (private characters and wiki edits share one bucket)
  • "Optimize" mode: when enabled, the AI may overwrite existing content (not just empties); use it when you want a polish pass on what you wrote
  • "Generate all" precondition: name + bio + relationship must be filled, or the button is greyed out
  • Auto-draft: every AI run autosaves a draft so closing the page won't lose your work

5.3 Drafts

While you edit, the form auto-saves:

  • Browser-side: in real time (close the tab, come back, still there)
  • Server-side: when you click AI generate or save manually
  • Recover at /my-drafts → click a draft → resume editing via ?draftId=...

5.4 Submitting

Before the "Submit" button you must provide:

  • Edit summary: a short note describing your change. ≥10 characters is mandatory for high-risk edits (lifecycle, delete, revert)
  • Mark as minor (isMinor): tick this if it's just typo/format/whitespace — patrollers process minor edits faster
⚠️ Edit-conflict detection

If someone else submitted an edit to the same entry while you were editing, you'll get a conflict prompt at submission. The system tracks baseRevisionId to detect this — you'll need to reopen the latest version and merge manually.

5.5 What happens after submit

Depends on your role tier:

TierLow-risk editsHigh-risk edits
newcomerEnters review queueEnters review queue
autoconfirmedGoes live immediatelyEnters review queue (still requires ≥10-char summary)
patroller / adminGoes live immediatelyGoes live immediately

5.6 Editing an existing entry

The ✏️ "Edit" button on a character page opens /character/:id/edit. The form is pre-filled with the latest revision (including pending fields, only visible to you); the rest of the flow matches creation.

6My private characters User

In addition to public wiki entries, every user has a private workspace: "My private characters" at /my-characters.

Differences vs wiki entries

FeatureWiki entryPrivate character
VisibilityPublicYou only
Review needed?Yes (for newcomers)No
Version history?YesNo
Use caseBuilding the public libraryPersonal creation / experimentation / backup

Core actions

  • Create: /my-characters/new, identical form to wiki edit but submission is instant
  • Edit / delete: button on each row in the list
  • Export JSON: dump the whole character (including recipe) as .json for backup or sharing
  • Import JSON: load from a file — choose "create new" or "overwrite existing"
  • Sync from wiki: copy any public entry into your private workspace to continue iterating
💡 Tip

Private characters + AI generation = the fastest "trial-and-error" loop. Polish privately, then push back to the public library when ready.

7History & diff User

Every change to a public entry is permanently retained, and any past revision can be inspected.

History tab

Character page → "History" tab → reverse-chronological revision list. Each row shows:

  • Revision ID
  • Editor's username
  • Timestamp
  • Edit summary
  • Operation (create / edit / delete request / restore request / revert)
  • Risk level (low / medium / high)
  • Patrolled? Reviewed?

Diff comparison

Tick any two revisions → "Compare" → opens /character/:id/diff?from=A&to=B with side-by-side rendering:

  • Which fields changed (diffFromParent)
  • Old vs new for each changed field
  • Recipe JSON structural diff (collapsible)

8Watchlist & recent changes User

8.1 Watch

The ⭐ button on a character page toggles watch. Once watched:

  • New edits to that entry appear in your watchlist feed
  • You can configure per-character flags: edit-notifications (notifyOnEdit) and talk-notifications (notifyOnTalk)

8.2 My watchlist

Sidebar → "My watchlist" (/watchlist). Two-pane layout:

  • Left: every character you watch, with an "unwatch" button per row; deleted characters get a deleted badge
  • Right: aggregated activity feed — both edit revisions and new talk threads

8.3 Recent changes

Sidebar → "Recent changes" (/recent-changes) — site-wide timeline visible to everyone:

  • All revisions on all entries (not limited to your watch list)
  • Lifecycle requests (delete / restore) and reverts included
  • Each row shows risk level, operation type, and patrol status
💡 For patrollers

Patrollers get an additional "show only unpatrolled" filter on this page — used for daily patrol work.

9Talk pages User

Every entry has its own talk page (like Wikipedia's "talk" tab) for discussing "how should we write this character" — not for chatting with the character.

Core actions

  • New thread: title + body, opens a new discussion
  • Reply: any post can be replied to (nested via parentPostId)
  • Delete your own posts: soft delete (record kept, content hidden)
  • Report a post: see the reporting section

Patroller actions

  • Lock thread (isLocked): prevent further replies — useful when discussions get heated
  • Mark resolved (isResolved): indicates consensus has been reached

10Reporting content User

Any signed-in user can report problematic content. Three target types:

  • Wiki revision: a specific edit introduced a problem (vandalism / false info / griefing)
  • Talk post: harassment / spam in discussions
  • Wiki page: the whole entry shouldn't exist (copyright / serious violation)

How to report

  1. Find the "🚩 Report" button next to the target content
  2. Pick a reason (spam / false info / abuse / other)
  3. Optionally add details (≤500 chars)
  4. Submit

Reports flow into the admin /admin/reports queue for human handling. Three outcomes:

  • Resolved: admin took action (delete / revert / block / etc.)
  • Dismissed: admin found the report invalid
  • Open: still pending
⚠️ Note

Bad-faith reporting can itself be acted on — repeated invalid reports trigger admin attention.

The platform has 4 role tiers, low to high:

TierUnlocked byPermissions

All public-entry edits enter the review queue.
autoconfirmedAuto-promoted by the system based on edit count and tenureLow-risk edits go live immediately. High-risk (lifecycle changes etc.) still need review.
patrollerGranted manually by an admin+ Access pending-review queue, approve / reject / request changes.
+ Mark patrols on Recent Changes.

+ Abuse-filter configuration.

How do I get to autoconfirmed?

The system promotes you automatically when these conditions are met (exact thresholds are site config):

  • Approved-edit count (approvedEditCount) ≥ threshold
  • Account age ≥ threshold
  • Reverted count (revertedCount) below threshold

Once met, the system sets autoconfirmedAt and the upgrade is instant. You can see this timestamp in account settings.

12Patroller handbook Patroller

Patrollers are the platform's first line of quality defense. There are three kinds of work: reviewing pending edits, patrolling already-published edits, and reverting bad changes.

12.1 Pending-review queue

Sidebar → "Pending reviews" (/pending-reviews, visible only to patrollers and above).

Queue fields:

  • Submitter / submission time / target entry
  • Operation type (create / edit / delete / restore / revert)
  • Risk level (low / medium / high — high requires ≥10-char summary)
  • Revision kind (latest / pending / etc.)
  • Edit summary
  • Diff preview link

Actions:

ActionEffect
ApproveEdit merges into stable; visible to all users
RejectEdit is discarded; not auto-synced to latest (submitter must redo)
Request changesLeave a note; submitter revises and resubmits
💡 Patrol pace tip

Prioritize "high-risk + long edit summary" submissions (usually serious large changes). Low-risk small fixes can be batched through quickly.

12.2 Patrol Recent Changes

"Patrol" and "approve" are two different things:

  • Approval (review): makes a pending edit go live — for newcomer submissions
  • Patrol: marks an already-live edit as "I've seen it, it's fine" — for autoconfirmed submissions (these are already live but still need a sanity check)

On /recent-changes, switch to "show only unpatrolled" and walk through. Click "Mark as patrolled" on any clean edit.

12.3 Revert

On the History tab, every past revision has a "Revert to this revision" button:

  1. Click → modal asks for a revert reason
  2. Submit → creates a new revision with operation: "revert" rolling content back to the target
  3. If you're a patroller/admin, the new revision goes live immediately; otherwise it'd enter the review queue (though that path isn't exposed to non-patrollers in practice)
⚠️ Note

Reverts are recorded against the original submitter's revertedCount and affect future promotion. Only revert when you're confident the edit is malicious or harmful — for differences of opinion, use the talk page.

13Admin handbook Admin

🛡️ Admin only

All /admin/* pages return 404 to non-admin accounts.

13.1 User & permission management

Visit /admin/users. Capabilities:

  • Browse the user list (username, type, role, registered date, edit stats)
  • Change any user's role tier (newcomer ↔ autoconfirmed ↔ patroller ↔ admin)
  • Inspect each user's stats: total edits, approved edits, reverted count, patrol count, last edit time, autoconfirmed-at

You can't change your own role tier (prevents accidentally locking yourself out).

13.2 Blocks

Visit /admin/blocks. Blocks prevent users from doing specific things:

ScopeEffect
globalSite-wide silence: can't edit, post, or report
pageCan't edit a specified entry only
talkCan't post in talk; editing still allowed

Each block record holds: user / scope / reason / created-by / created-at / expires-at (expiresAt, optional — empty means permanent) / revoked-by / revoked-at. The list page splits "active" and "expired/revoked".

13.3 Page protection

Visit /admin/protection. Adds an editing barrier to a single entry:

Page-level protection

LevelWho can edit
noneAll signed-in users (default)
semiAutoconfirmed and above — blocks newcomers
fullPatroller and above — trusted users only

Review policy

  • open: edits go live immediately, no forced review
  • pending_changes: every edit is held pending approval

Field-level protection

Set the minimum role required to edit a specific field (e.g. name):

fieldPath: "name"
minRoleToEdit: "patroller"

Useful for locking down vandal-prone fields (character name, relationship type) while keeping the rest collaboratively editable.

All protection changes are logged: oldLevel → newLevel, changed-by, reason, expires-at — fully traceable.

13.4 Reports queue

Visit /admin/reports. Handles user reports:

  • Grouped by status: open / resolved / dismissed
  • Each shows: reporter, target type (revision / post / page), reason, details, timestamp
  • Action: change status to resolved or dismissed (the actual remediation — delete, revert, block — happens in the other admin tools)

13.5 Abuse filters

Visit /admin/abuse-filters. Regex-based content interception rules:

FieldNotes
nameRule name (admin-only)
patternRegular expression
scopeWhere it applies (edits / talk / all)
actionflag (mark for review) / reject (block submission) / log (record only)
severitylow / medium / high — affects prioritization

The page also shows hit statistics: how many times each rule has fired, hit-user distribution, and the latest N hit details.

💡 Regex tip

Run new rules with action: log for a week to gauge hit scope, then promote to flag or reject once you've confirmed there are no false positives.

13.6 Governance dashboard

Visit /admin/wiki-stats. One-stop overview:

  • Live counters: today's creates, this week's creates, pending-queue length
  • User-quality leaderboards: most-reverted, low-approval-rate, watch-list users
  • Daily trends: creates, approves, rejects, abuse-filter hits, autoconfirmed promotions
  • Filter performance: per-rule hit rate, false-positive rate (manual reversal count)

14FAQ

Q: I submitted an edit — where did it go? Why is the page still old?

Newcomer edits enter a review queue. You see your own changes (the "current" view), but other visitors see the most recently approved "stable" version. Once a patroller approves your edit, everyone sees it. Check /recent-changes to confirm your submission landed.

Q: Why is the AI generate button greyed out?

Three possibilities:

  • "Generate all" requires name + bio + relationship to be filled first
  • You've used your hourly quota (15/user/hour, shared between private and wiki)
  • Every field is already filled and you didn't enable optimize mode — by default the AI only fills empties

Q: Why am I seeing different content than someone else?

You're on the "current" view (with your or someone's pending edits); other visitors who haven't signed in or aren't part of the edit see the "stable" view (latest approved). This isolation is by design — unreviewed content isn't shown to the public.

Q: How do I get promoted to autoconfirmed?

Keep doing high-quality edits — the system auto-promotes you based on "approved-edit count + account age + low revert rate". No application needed. Account settings shows your current edit stats.

Q: Can others see my private characters?

No. Private characters appear only in your /my-characters list — even admins don't see them in the back-end. The only way to expose one is "sync to wiki".

Q: What if I hit an edit conflict?

The system catches it at submission and pops a warning. Easiest workflow: copy your draft to clipboard → close the edit page → reopen it (you'll get the latest version) → manually merge your changes back in → resubmit.

Q: I forgot my password — what now?

On the login page choose "Email code" — sign in that way, then change your password in account settings. This requires that you bound an email when registering. If you didn't, the account can't be self-recovered; contact an admin.

Q: Can I use the exported character JSON elsewhere?

Yes — the export is a complete recipe + content-snapshot in the platform's internal format. You can import it into any account on this platform. In theory other tools could consume it too, but they need to support this format.

Q: I was blocked — how do I appeal?

The block record shows the reason and the admin. See the next section for appeal channels.

15Feedback

Hit a bug? Want to suggest something? Disagree with a moderation decision? Channels:

  • On-site discussion: open a new thread on a relevant entry's talk page (good for content questions)
  • Reports: use the 🚩 button for specific bad content
  • Email / community: contact details to be added by admins before public launch
💡 How to write good feedback

Include: specific page URL, what you did, what you expected, what actually happened, browser & OS. One report like that beats ten "doesn't work"s.
